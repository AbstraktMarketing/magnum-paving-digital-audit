The Local SEO Problem — In One Paragraph

Magnum Paving's directory presence is fragmented and partially outdated. Of 21 directories scanned, only 4 show the correct Tucson address (EZlocal, HotFrog, Cylex, YP.com). Three others — USCity.net, ShowMeLocal, and Opendi — still list 1165 N Anita Ave, a former location. When Google's local algorithm cross-references these conflicting addresses for the same business, it weakens the trust signal and suppresses local pack rankings. Meanwhile, Bing is completely absent — a critical miss since Bing powers Apple Maps, Amazon Alexa, and many business navigation tools. Facebook has no listing either, eliminating social discovery. Of the remaining 12 not-present directories (n49, TripAdvisor, Hours.com, Navmii, and others), each is a missed citation that competitors are likely using to build authority. With 4 correct listings, 4 problematic, and 12 missing entirely, Magnum Paving's local footprint is less than 20% built — and what exists sends mixed signals to Google.
